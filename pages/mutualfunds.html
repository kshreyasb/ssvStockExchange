Mutual Funds Information

1. Mutual Fund

A type of investment vehicle consisting of a pool of funds collected from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, and other assets.

2. Net Asset Value (NAV)

The per-share market value of all the securities held by a mutual fund, calculated daily after the market closes.

3. Expense Ratio

The percentage of a mutual fund's assets deducted annually to cover management fees, administrative expenses, and other costs.

4. Load and No-Load Funds

Load Funds: Mutual funds that charge a sales commission or fee when shares are bought or sold.
No-Load Funds: Mutual funds that do not charge a sales commission.

5. Asset Allocation

The distribution of a mutual fund's assets among different asset classes, such as stocks, bonds, and cash equivalents, to achieve diversification.

6. Diversification

Spreading investments across various securities to reduce risk.

7. Prospectus

A legal document provided by mutual funds to potential investors, containing information about the fund's objectives, risks, fees, and performance.

8. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

An investment strategy allowing investors to contribute a fixed amount regularly (monthly or quarterly) to a mutual fund.

9. Redemption

The process of selling mutual fund shares back to the fund.

10. Dividend Reinvestment Plan (DRIP)

An option allowing investors to automatically reinvest mutual fund dividends to purchase additional shares.

11. Index Fund

A type of mutual fund that aims to replicate the performance of a specific market index.

12. Active vs. Passive Management

Active Management: Mutual funds managed by fund managers who make decisions to outperform the market.
Passive Management: Index funds that aim to replicate the performance of a specific market index.

13. 12b-1 Fee

A fee that covers a mutual fund's marketing and distribution costs.

14. Redemption Fee

A fee charged when investors sell mutual fund shares, often applied to discourage frequent trading.

15. Capital Gains Distribution

The distribution of profits made by a mutual fund's portfolio manager to the fund's shareholders.

16. Target-Date Fund

A mutual fund with a diversified portfolio that automatically adjusts its asset allocation over time, becoming more conservative as the target date (such as retirement) approaches.

17. Risk Tolerance

An investor's ability to endure fluctuations in the value of their investments.

18. Redemption Period

The time it takes for a mutual fund to process and fulfill a redemption request.

19. Redemption Fee

A fee charged when investors sell mutual fund shares, often applied to discourage frequent trading.

20. Morningstar Rating

A rating system by Morningstar that assesses mutual funds based on historical performance, fees, and risk.

Additional Information

  • Mutual Fund Manager: The person or team responsible for managing a mutual fund's investment portfolio.
  • Lipper Average: A benchmark used to compare the performance of mutual funds in the same category.
  • Turnover Ratio: The percentage of a mutual fund's holdings that are bought or sold in a given year.
  • Front-End Load: A sales charge applied when purchasing mutual fund shares.
  • Back-End Load: A sales charge applied when selling mutual fund shares, often declining over time.
  • Money Market Fund: A type of mutual fund that invests in short-term, low-risk securities.
  • Qualified Dividend: Dividends that qualify for a lower tax rate.

Investors should carefully read the prospectus and consider their investment goals and risk tolerance before investing in mutual funds.
